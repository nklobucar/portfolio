Bosnia i Herzegovina

Preservation, Reconciliation, and cevapcici

Leaving the Adriatic behind, I climb into the Dinaric Alps toward the Bosniak heartland. My first destination is Mostar, an ancient city rooted in Ottoman tradition, known for the Stari Most Bridge.

Just 20km from Mostar lies the Blagaj Monastery. Built around 1520 during the Ottoman period, it served as a residence for the Dervish cults and their leaders. It is situated at the source of the Buna River, which flows out of a cave beneath the cliffs. Dervishes are followers of a mystical Islamic tradition known for their ascetic and spiritual practices.
In the village of Gnojnice outside of Mostar, built into a hill, is an abandoned Yugoslav military hangar. Once a top-secret facility under Tito, it was discovered in the 1990s during the war and now lies derilict.
After a short drive winding through the hills and valleys of Herzegovina, we stop at the historic village of Počitelj. Its highest point hosts a 13th century fort, used by the Ottomans as a frontier post against the Austro-Hungarians.
After spending 4 days exploring the region of Herzegovina, I take the (4 euro) train northeast to the largest and capital city of Bosnia; Sarajevo. Known as one of the most scenic routes in Europe, it did not disappoint.
During the Yugoslav wars in the 1990s, Sarajevo was held under siege for over 4 years, the longest siege of a capital city in modern history. The scars of these atrocities are still seen today.
Despite this turbulant history, Sarajevo's unique character reflects the Bosniak's strong adherence to Eastern Ottoman tradition, in an area surrounded by christianity.
Another unique landmark of Sarajevo is the abandonded bobsled track. Located in the surrounding mountains, it hosted athletes during the Sarajevo 1984 Olympics. Parts were subsequently destroyed in the conflict 10 years later, however most still stands today. The Gondola was rebuilt in 2019.
Follow along with my journey, heading southeast through the mountains, towards the Bay of Kotor in Montenegro.
